Welcome

Hello! My name is Vivek Ganesh!

I’ve studied finance, managed stockrooms, built marketing campaigns, and somewhere along the way, I found out what I really love is this: decoding people.

My journey started in Hyderabad, where I completed my B.Com in Computers, diving deep into business statistics, SQL, and the logic of spreadsheets. During that time, I took up a job at Amazon as a Customer Service Associate. That headset and ticket system taught me more about empathy, escalation, and brand loyalty than any textbook ever could. I didn’t know it at the time, but that experience would light the path forward.

After a brief stint as a Financial Analyst at Deloitte, I made an unexpected turn. I left the formulas behind and joined the chaos of creative work. At 3DM and later To The New, I managed B2B and B2C campaigns for brands like Hilti and STL.Tech, driving growth across India and the APAC region. I learned how to shape insight into strategy, how to pitch without the buzzwords, and how performance marketing really works when humans come first.

That calling brought me to London, where I completed an MSc in Marketing at Queen Mary University. I immersed myself in customer journey mapping, digital analytics, and behaviour-driven growth. More importantly, I unlearned the idea that marketing is fluff. It's anything but. It’s data, timing, empathy, and intention stitched together.

Today, I work at UNIQLO as a Loss Prevention Supervisor. It may seem like a detour in a marketer’s story, but it has taught me to see the floor like a funnel. I’ve helped reduce shrinkage, optimise product displays through behavioural observation, and turn a high-loss store into a high-performance one. It’s strategic, fast-paced, and grounded in real-time decision-making.

When I’m not working, you’ll find me binge-watching anime, discussing life with ChatGPT and Gemini at 1am, or overanalysing Mad Men scenes like they’re case studies. I love street-side chai as much as I love campaign pitch decks. My camera roll is full of ad screenshots. And yes, I once paused a movie to rewatch the product placement.

This site isn’t just a portfolio. It’s a collection of the people, problems, platforms, and late-night ideas that shaped me.
