Graphic Design

Engaging Graphics for Social Media Accounts

Using Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Figma and Canva

Graphics are a social media staple that enable more audience attention than plain text. They help visually display and communicate a brand’s recognizable mood in an aesthetic way, encourage users to digest the eyecatching content at hand and remember it.

Context: I designed graphics for Preview Careers, SFU Business Administration Student Society, BASS LAUNCH, SFU Wish Youth Network Society and Learning Disabilities Career Conference within holistic marketing roles.

Key Considerations:

• The organization’s brand guidelines and key message, aligned with the marketing campaign’s goals and past content, ensuring consistency

• The audience’s needs, language and behaviour, ensuring content is relevant, relatable and optimized, as analytics can show, for example, that past content posted at 9:00 pm show higher engagement, meaning that posting at 9:00 pm can be effective for future content posts

• The balance of text, white space and colours on the graphic and/or caption

• The image dimensions and resolution, suited to the right social media platform as graphics that take up the most space on a smartphone can draw more audience attention and square images on Instagram are preferred aesthetically

Steps:

1. Developing my Adobe InDesign, Illustrator and Photoshop skills through self-directed learning via online sources such as Youtube tutorials, alongside mentorship

2. Generating content and design ideas through analyzing similar brands and content on a similar topic

3. Designing content leveraging free stock photos, graphics and icons from sites including Pexels, Freepik and Flaticon

4. Analyzing past results and conducting research to ensure the right timing, hashtags and components like location tags are used, to generate more awareness and engagement

5. Publishing content via the right channel at the right time, planned through an editorial calendar and platform like Hootsuite or Planoly
